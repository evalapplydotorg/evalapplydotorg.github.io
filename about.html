Standing Invitation 4

Email me just because (not just for work). Whatever feeds your curiosity; silly, fun, nerdy, serious 5.

Why?

  1. Do unto me what I have done unto others. I habitually cold-email anyone who moves me in some way (joy, insight, utility, mind shift…). I also love to receive such email!
  2. There is too little unsolicited positive feedback in much of most of our lives. At some point in the fuzzy past, I decided enough was enough. At least someone somewhere ought to feel good sometime for no reason whatsoever. Since that realisation, I have cold-emailed people willy-nilly. See also: Saying Thank You, and Days Are Easily Made.
  3. It's always been a delight; no regrets. You should try it too!

So, let serendipity reign; write away!

A statutory content warning, though… A friend told me I can get along with a rock. AC/DC started playing in my head. So, dear human, that is whom you are about to email 6.

Like yin/yang ☯, they contain each other. And they evoke other nonduals to live by; diffuse/focus, abstract/reify, design/develop.

    Stuart Feldman: If nothing else, Lisp was carefully defined in terms of Lisp.

    Alan Kay: Yes, that was the big revelation to me when I was in graduate school—when I finally understood that the half page of code on the bottom of page 13 of the Lisp 1.5 manual was Lisp in itself. These were “Maxwell’s Equations of Software!” This is the whole world of programming in a few lines that I can put my hand over.
